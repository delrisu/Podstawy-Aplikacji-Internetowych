Spice & Wolf is a combination of genres that somehow brings out the best of all it's elements. It's a low fantasy set in a medieval style world, where the magic that does exist is relatively small and far between; basically Holo, a harvet goddess, is the only thing that would set this story apart from a more plain historical setting.

I had watched the first season of the anime and while that's actually a quite good adaptation and a lot of fun; it simply can't fully do the story justice. The verbal sparing and internal calculations and decisions that Lawrence makes are much more apparent on the written page; and serve to deepen the connection that he and Holo are developing.

While the first several volumes do deal a good bit with certain aspects of commerce; this ends up setting the stage not just for Lawrence's personality and state of mind; but also helps expand on the world the story takes place in (it's pretty cold and cruel in many respects).

The writing and translation are clear, and in places practically lyrical. You have a blend of low fantasy, the early buds of a potential romance, and a commerce deal gone bad. There are no dragons threatening to destroy towns, nor mighty army's clashing. Instead you have the a story that simply does some quite world building by way of the characters interactions and relationships.
